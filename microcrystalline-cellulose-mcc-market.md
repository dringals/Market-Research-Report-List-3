<p><p>Microcrystalline cellulose (MCC) is a versatile ingredient used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and cosmetics. MCC can be categorized into two main types: wood pulp based and refined cotton based. Wood pulp based MCC is derived from wood pulp while refined cotton based MCC is made from refined cotton. Both types of MCC have similar properties and are used as thickeners, stabilizers, and bulking agents in a wide range of products due to their excellent binding and emulsifying properties.</p></p>
