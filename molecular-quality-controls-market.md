<p><ul><li>Independent</li><li>Instrument Specific</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Molecular quality controls can be categorized into two main market types: independent and instrument-specific. Independent quality controls are designed to be compatible with a wide range of instruments and are produced by third-party manufacturers. On the other hand, instrument-specific quality controls are tailored to work with a specific type or brand of instrument, ensuring accurate and reliable performance. Both types of quality controls play a crucial role in maintaining the accuracy and consistency of molecular diagnostic tests in clinical laboratories.</p></p>
