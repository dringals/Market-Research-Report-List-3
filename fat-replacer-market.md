<p><ul><li>Carbohydrate-based</li><li>Protein-based</li><li>Lipid-based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fat replacers are categorized into three main types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based. Carbohydrate-based fat replacers are typically derived from sources like starches and fibers, providing a similar texture to fats. Protein-based fat replacers, on the other hand, are often made from sources like whey or soy, offering a creamy texture. Lipid-based fat replacers are created from substances like oils and emulsifiers, providing a rich mouthfeel similar to traditional fats. Each type of fat replacer caters to different consumer preferences and dietary needs.</p></p>
