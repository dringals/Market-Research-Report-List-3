<p><p>Synthetic dental bone graft substitutes come in various types, with the two most common being hydroxyapatite and tricalcium phosphate. Hydroxyapatite is a calcium phosphate compound that closely resembles natural bone, providing a scaffold for new bone growth. Tricalcium phosphate is another calcium-based material that also promotes bone regeneration. Both materials are biocompatible and are widely used in dental procedures to fill bone defects and enhance bone healing. The market for these synthetic dental bone graft substitutes continues to grow due to their efficacy and safety.</p></p>
