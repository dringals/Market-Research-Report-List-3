<p><p>Application Processing Units (APUs) come in various market types based on the number of cores they have. Single-core APUs have a single processing core, suitable for basic tasks. Dual-core APUs have two cores, offering better performance for multitasking. Quad-core APUs have four cores, providing even higher processing power for more demanding applications. Hexa-core APUs have six cores, offering optimal performance for advanced computing tasks. Octa-core APUs, with eight cores, deliver the highest processing power and are ideal for intensive applications such as gaming or video editing.</p></p>
