<p><p>Automatic Dependent Surveillance Broadcast (ADS-B) is a surveillance technology used in aviation for tracking aircraft. There are three main types of ADS-B markets: ADS-B Out, which transmits information from the aircraft to ground stations and other aircraft; ADS-B In, which receives information from other aircraft and ground stations; and ADS-B Ground Stations, which receive information from aircraft and relay it to air traffic control. These markets work together to improve aviation safety and efficiency through enhanced surveillance and communication capabilities.</p></p>
