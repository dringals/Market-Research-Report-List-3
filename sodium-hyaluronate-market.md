<p><ul><li>Microbial Fermentation</li><li>Animal Tissue Extraction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sodium Hyaluronate is produced through two main methods: Microbial Fermentation and Animal Tissue Extraction. In microbial fermentation, bacteria or yeast are used to produce hyaluronic acid in a controlled environment. This method is cost-effective and produces high-quality sodium hyaluronate. On the other hand, animal tissue extraction involves extracting hyaluronic acid from rooster combs or other animal tissues. Although this method has been traditionally used, it is less preferred today due to ethical concerns and the risk of disease transmission.</p></p>
