<p><p>Linear strain gauges are devices used to measure the deformation of an object under stress. There are two main types of linear strain gauges: resistive strain gauges and fiber optic strain gauges. Resistive strain gauges work by changing their resistance in response to mechanical deformation, while fiber optic strain gauges use changes in light intensity to measure strain. Both types are commonly used in a variety of industries such as aerospace, automotive, and civil engineering for monitoring and testing structural integrity.</p></p>
