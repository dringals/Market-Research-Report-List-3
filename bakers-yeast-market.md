<p><p>Baker's Yeast market is divided into two main types: Active Dry Yeast and Inactive Dry Yeast. Active Dry Yeast is a form of yeast that is alive and actively fermenting, providing leavening action in baked goods. It must be rehydrated before use. Inactive Dry Yeast, on the other hand, is yeast that has been deactivated through heat and is used primarily as a flavoring agent in foods. Both types play a crucial role in the baking industry, with active dry yeast being more commonly used for leavening purposes.</p></p>
