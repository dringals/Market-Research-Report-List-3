<p><p>Implantable Ocular Drug Delivery Systems refer to devices that are surgically implanted into the eye to release drugs over an extended period. These systems offer targeted and sustained drug delivery, eliminating the need for frequent injections. On the other hand, Particulate Drug Delivery Systems involve the use of microscopic particles to deliver drugs to specific regions of the eye. These systems provide controlled release and improve drug efficiency by targeting the affected area. Both types of ocular drug delivery systems aim to enhance the effectiveness and convenience of treatment for various eye conditions.</p></p>
